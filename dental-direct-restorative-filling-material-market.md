<p><p>The dental direct restorative filling material market comprises several types, including composite fillings, amalgam fillings, glass ionomer fillings, and others. Composite fillings are tooth-colored materials that blend well with natural teeth, ideal for aesthetic restorations. Amalgam fillings, a durable combination of metals, are commonly used for posterior teeth due to their strength. Glass ionomer fillings release fluoride and bond chemically to teeth, making them suitable for areas needing decay protection. Other materials include resin ionomers and various innovative substances catering to specific dental needs.</p></p>
