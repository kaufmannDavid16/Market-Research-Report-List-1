<p><p>Radiation detection in the industrial and scientific markets involves three main types: Geiger counters, scintillation detectors, and solid-state detectors. Geiger counters are widely used for their simplicity and effectiveness in measuring radiation levels, commonly employed in safety applications. Scintillation detectors utilize scintillating materials that emit light when exposed to radiation, offering high sensitivity for various isotopes. Solid-state detectors use semiconductor materials for precise measurements and are favored in laboratory settings for their accuracy and durability, catering to diverse radiation detection needs.</p></p>
