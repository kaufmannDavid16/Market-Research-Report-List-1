<p><p>Orthopedic surgery bone cements are specialized materials used to anchor implants in bone during surgical procedures, particularly in joint replacement and fracture fixation surgeries. These cements, primarily polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA), offer mechanical stability and help in preventing loosening of implants over time. The market for orthopedic surgery bone cements is experiencing significant growth due to an aging population and increasing incidences of orthopedic conditions, such as osteoporosis and arthritis. </p><p>Additionally, advancements in surgical techniques and materials are enhancing the effectiveness and safety of these cements, driving further demand. <p><ul><li>Low Viscosity</li><li>Medium Viscosity</li><li>High Viscosity</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The orthopedic surgery bone cements market is categorized into three types based on viscosity: low, medium, and high viscosity. Low viscosity cements are designed for easy flow and penetration into bone cavities, ideal for minimally invasive procedures. Medium viscosity cements offer a balance between handling and flow, suitable for various orthopedic applications. High viscosity cements provide superior mechanical strength and stability, often used in load-bearing situations. Each type serves specific surgical needs, influencing material selection and procedural outcomes.</p></p>
