<p><p>The PVB (polyvinyl butyral) resins market is categorized into high viscosity and low viscosity types, based on their fluidity and application suitability. High viscosity PVB resins are typically used in applications requiring thicker films, such as automotive and architectural glazing, where enhanced adhesion and impact resistance are crucial. Low viscosity PVB resins, on the other hand, are favored for applications needing smoother coatings and easier processing, including ink and paint formulations. Each type serves distinct industries and functional needs.</p></p>
