<p><p>Intra-Abdominal Pressure (IAP) measurement devices are categorized into two main types: muscle-based and abdomen-based devices. Muscle-based devices utilize sensors or intravaginal pressure techniques to assess pressure changes within the abdominal cavity indirectly. In contrast, abdomen-based devices typically employ direct measurement techniques, such as catheter systems or pressure transducers, that gauge IAP through the abdominal wall. Both types aim to provide accurate monitoring for critical care patients, enhancing clinical decision-making in conditions influenced by intra-abdominal pressure fluctuations.</p></p>
