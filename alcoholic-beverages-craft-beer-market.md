<p><p>The craft beer market is predominantly divided into two main types: ales and lagers. Ales are characterized by top fermentation, resulting in a variety of flavors, aromas, and higher alcohol content, often showcasing fruity and complex profiles. Lagers, on the other hand, utilize bottom fermentation, leading to a cleaner, crisper taste with a smoother finish. Both categories cater to diverse consumer preferences, contributing significantly to the growing popularity of craft beers in the alcoholic beverages market.</p></p>
