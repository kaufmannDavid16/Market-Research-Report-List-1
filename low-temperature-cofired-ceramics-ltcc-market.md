<p><p>Low Temperature Cofired Ceramics (LTCC) are advanced materials used primarily in the electronics sector, particularly for creating multilayer structures that integrate passive components such as resistors, capacitors, and inductors. LTCC technology stands out due to its ability to allow the co-firing of dielectric and metal layers at low temperatures, making it suitable for various substrates, including ceramics and composite materials. This capability leads to enhanced reliability and compact designs in electronic devices.</p><p>The LTCC market is witnessing significant growth, driven by rising demand for miniaturized electronic components in sectors such as telecommunications, automotive, and medical devices. The increasing adoption of wireless communication technologies and IoT applications further fuels market expansion, as LTCC provides excellent performance in high-frequency environments.
