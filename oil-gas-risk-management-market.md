<p><p>Oil and gas risk management involves two primary market types: Initial Risk Management and Residual Risk Management. Initial Risk Management focuses on identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks before they materialize by implementing preventive measures and strategies. This ensures a safer operational environment and minimizes potential losses. In contrast, Residual Risk Management deals with risks that remain after initial measures are taken. This involves monitoring, reassessing, and developing contingency plans to handle any residual risks effectively, ensuring overall industry resilience.</p></p>
